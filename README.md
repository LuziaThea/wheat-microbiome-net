# Abstract
Plant-associated microbiomes comprise diverse microbial species that coexist and interact, influencing community structure and host plant health. However, our understanding of these interactions in field conditions, particularly at the species and strain level, remains limited. This knowledge gap hinders the development of effective biocontrol applications, as laboratory findings often fail to translate to field settings due to insufficient comprehension of in situ interaction networks. This study addresses this limitation by employing taxon-specific high-resolution amplicons to elucidate a cross-kingdom co-occurrence network within the wheat phyllosphere microbiome. We performed in-depth monitoring of strains from the hub genus Pseudomonas, revealing a high degree of strain-specificity of Pseudomonas interactions both within and across kingdoms. Through systematic analysis of negative interactions, we identified a consortium of ten biocontrol taxa with the potential to suppress seven fungal pathogens, along with additional biocontrol stabilizer strains that may enhance persistence and effectiveness in field conditions. We validated the strain-specific interactions of Pseudomonas with different strains of the major fungal pathogen Zymoseptoria tritici using co-inoculation experiments involving Pseudomonas strains and Z. tritici isolates from the same field. Consistent with our prediction, we identified a Pseudomonas poae isolate as the most antagonistic towards Z. tritici both in vitro and in planta. Our study demonstrates the potential of taxon-specific high-resolution network inference in elucidating microbial interaction networks. Specifically, we show that this approach can resolve strain-specific interaction patterns of biocontrol candidates with high persistence in field conditions, while also identifying potential biocontrol stabilizer strains. These insights into strain-specific dynamics can support the design of more effective biocontrol strategies.
